Project Overview

Challenge:

Create a responsive site for this organization in order to facilitate a better experience for stakeholders and address the challenges of encroachment.

Solution:

Design and implement responsive site that provides the user with the access to primer series content.

Role:

Content strategy, Research, Information Architecture, Web Development

Tool:

Atom, HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, AWS

Discovery

My team and I were given the daunting task of transforming a fairly lengthy document/static webpage into an interactive user-friendly site. Design thinking was an important activity done to help prioritize important aspects of the site. The site was very content heavy, so minimizing the amount of content would improve the cognitive load for the user.

Iterative design helped in defining the necessary features required to successfully navigate through the website. Since content is literally the most imporant thing on the site, it was important to find different ways to display the content on the site without overwelming the user. We considered incorporating functionality such as: tabs, carousels, pagination, and images with hover/click overlays in order to achieve this goal. The greatest benefit of using such functionality is that they enable more than one piece of content to occupy the same piece of prime real estate.

The Results

After several iteratives of redesigning the webpage and going back and forth with the client, the site went live in October 2017. Once it is live for a few months, and we get a good amount of feedback from users, we will continue our discussions about additional web and mobile features that may be added to a future launch. The final product can be seen here.

This preview to the right of this text highlights a few of the features implemented on the site. Such as: paralax scrolling effects, animations, a left hand navigation and top navigation. The top navigation is placed there to ensure the user is aware of the name of the section that they are on within the site, without having to hover over the current chapter in the left hand navigation.
