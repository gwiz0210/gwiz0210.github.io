Project Overview

Challenge:

Often companies suffer from high turnover rates due to poor culture fit. There needs to be a better way to connect employers more personally to their job candidates.

Solution:

Create an application in which job seekers and employers are matched based on culture-fit compatibility.

Role:

Researcher, Interviewer, Information Architect, End-to-End Experience Designer (sketch, wireframe, iterate, clickable prototype)

Tool:

Pen, Paper, Stickies, Sketch, Invision, Keynote

Background

I took a 10 week User Experience Design course with General Assembly. This course gave me more in depth knowledge on user design practices.

Throughout the course I worked on Cultiv8, a platform that connected employers with job seekers based on the culture fit compatibility. Both job seekers and employers are given a set of questions that will match them. Machine learning mechanisms are used through out the interaction with the system in order to enhance the culture fit profile.

Research & Synthesis

Initially, my goal was to connect employers to job seekers based on the personality of the job seeker. I conducted interviews with various HR professionals only. However, I expanded my criteria after the first 5 interviews. It was clear to me that the perspective of job seekers was also essential in order to fully grasp the user need for this product.

I found that interviewees were particularly not fond of the word "personality" when it came to recruiting and finding a job because they felt that it was not reliable. The word combinations "work culture" and "culture-fit" were mentioned very often during the interviews. It was important that the job seeker fits well within the company culture, and the company culture and values align well with the job seekers values. With this information, I was able to reframe my goals for this application.

Goals:

  • Improve the current methods of recruitment by effectively incorporating culture fit compatibility into the job application process.
  • Match candidates and employers based on cultural fit.

Sketches & Low-Fidelity Prototype

I used pen and paper for the very early stages of my design process. Sketching was very helpful and gave me time to cement my content architecture before diving too much into the details. The photos below include early sketches created during the discovery process. It shows the process of posting a job from the Human Resources professional's prospective on this application. The user will:

  1. Set up the job description details
  2. Pick desire personality characteristics of applicants
  3. Share the details of the job description with team members
  4. Team members approve/disapprove/comment/propose edits for the job description content
  5. Once changes are made and team approves, post the job on various job boards and social media
  6. Once applicants have applied for the job, view their profile and share it with team members

Results & Takeaways

User Testing Feedback:

After conducting user testing users provided very valuable feedback and suggestions that I will implement in my next iteration. Having a mobile application is one very popular suggestion I recieved from users. There was still much concern about the ways the culture-fit of the user is measured, so I added a social media aspect to the application in order to better analyze/enhance the users culture-fit profile.

Next Steps:
  • Find Developers (AI and Big Data experts) to implement project
  • Implement feedback from user testing
  • Create mockups for the hiring/recruiting process
  • Further research on methods of personality testing.
